Battle Creek Academy

This represents the web portion of an entire rebranding campaign the school underwent. The color scheme employs a rich blue and dark grey to create a very classy feel. The homepage is designed to present viewers with pertinent information for future events as well as inform them of recent school news. It also works to easily direct visitors to whatever section of the site they are seeking. The interior page maintains a very clean look, while utilizing a side navigation to facilitate quick travel between sub-pages within a section.

CSS Zen Garden

The idea behind the CSS Zen Garden project was to challenge designers to take their web designs to the next level with skillfully used HTML and CSS techniques. My design was based around a retro gym theme. It used hand-made grunge visuals to create a warm aged aesthetic. It also boasts mustachioed-muscle men to the strength of its CSS.

RTO Electronics

RTO Electronics was looking for an entire site redesign as well as a new logo. Their business was amatuer radio repair and sales. In demographic research, it became apparent that their site was mainly visited by clientele that were middle-aged or older and in many cases not well versed in newer technologies. To accomodate this we created a very simple three page navigation. We also used large, easily-readable fonts. The color scheme reflects that of the Heathkit company-the primary brand of radio that RTO Electronics repairs. We also created a custom back-end for them using Django.

Battle Creek Academy

This was an entire rebranding project for the Academy. I started off by developing a typographic logo, which had a very clean sophisticated aesthic. It was meant to invoke feelings of simplicity and order. As a college preparatory school, it was important that they appear professional and organized. This aesthetic along with the rich blue color were then used all their stationary as well as a sixteen page brochure I designed for them. The logo was complimented with clean, simple design to create an elegant look for their brand.

Gem Resources International

This logo design was for a international aid fund management company. The company provided support for other international charities worldwide. The core concept behind this logo redesign was the expanding influence over the world. The main circular shape represents the globe which is covered with expanding cocentric circles which show how one small event can influence another causing a chain reaction. This illustrates how each good deed encourages another.

Kindle: Band

This was a logo I created for a local band. I also designed a business card, sticker, button, and some t-shirts for them.There are a number of meanings in this logo. The first lies in the colors themselves. The warm orange and rich brown invoke feelings of comfort, acceptance, inclusion, hope, and love. Combined with the imagery of a sun rising above the kindle word mark, this feeling of hope and joy is made even stronger. Also inside the sun imagery, the negative space is employed to create an abstracted spark image. This combines nicely with the name itself to emphasize the idea of kindle. Throughout the logos simplicity, warmth, and clean imagery the ideas of the bands ministry and mission are communicated.

Eau Claire SDA Church

The concept behind this logo played off the fact that Eau Claire means "Clear Water." The logo uses a drop over the "i" to illustrate a person. This person, or drop, then creates waves throughout their community influencing those around them. This is the core concept behind the mission statement of the church which is to reach out to the community around it. From this logo I then designed business cards, letterhead, and envelopes, as well as a standards manual to inform the church on how to apply the logo.

Die Jager

This was a magazine project I art directed. The concept behind this project was a dark, angsty muscle magazine. With this in mind, I employed the help of a photographer to capture the imagery I wanted. I then designed a layout that the viewer would be drawn through by circles with connecting lines. The idea was to give it a feeling of motion. This was complimented with a dark gray texture and some highlight colors.

Merrell: Kick the Elements

This was an ad campaign I art directed to sell Merrell shoes. The slogan I concepted for the campaign, "Kick the Elements," was shown by the shoes kicking through water, air, and earth. To complete this goal, I worked with a photographer to capture the various elements in motion as well as stills of the shoe. I then composited the images together and added in text to create the final product.

Freshmint

My idea for this ad was to take the slogan of the mint, "Calibrated Coolness," and use hyperbole to sell it. So I decided to depict someone trying to cool their mouth off with a fire extinguisher, but then state there's a better way. The concept uses humor to draw the viewer in, and then push the concept that these mints are really cool and refreshing.

Back to Top

New York Times: The Shock of the Familiar

A while back the New York Times held a design competition for their cover based around the story "The Shock of the Familiar." This concept used a familiar - but shocking - face combined with a toothbrush which tied into their closing line, "From toothbrushes to web pages, a guided tour." The idea was to draw the viewers attention through imagery and a language that they would not be expecting on their news stand.
